    Registration

    Foreign nationals must register within 15 days of arrival in Uzbekistan. Hotels: Registration is typically done automatically at check-in. Confirm with the receptionist. Apartments: Ask your landlord, realtor, or adaptation specialist for help. Registration is done at the local passport office or police station based on the property owner's address. Keep your registration document — it may be required when leaving the country. Temporary registration is usually valid for six months and can be extended.

    Important!

    If you move from a hotel to a rented apartment, you must register on the same day you check out of the hotel to avoid an administrative fine. Be sure to collect your registration form from the hotel and submit it, along with the completed questionnaire, at the passport office. The same procedure applies when registering upon initial arrival.

    Mobile Network

    We issue a SIM card on the first working day. Just remind the adaptation specialist about this and bring your phone with IMEI. The SIM card will be needed to obtain a PINFL and open a bank card.

    Personal Identification (PINFL) in Uzbekistan

    Your adaptation specialist will assist you in obtaining your PINFL (Personal Identification Number of an Individual). Here is a brief overview of the process: To be registered with an Uzbek company, you are required to have a PINFL. This unique identifier is used to verify your identity across various services, including government, banking, and social services. Important: When receiving your PINFL, carefully check that your personal details — such as your full name and date of birth — are entered correctly. As part of the process, you will be photographed and fingerprinted. You will also be informed when your PINFL is ready for collection. Processing time typically ranges from 15–20 minutes to one business day, depending on system availability.

    Registration of Mobile Device's IMEI

    Foreigners visiting Uzbekistan who want to use a local SIM card must register the IMEI code of their mobile device. This is part of the national system (UZIMEI) to prevent phone theft and ensure proper use of the mobile network. If you use a foreign SIM (roaming), registration is not required for up to 60 days. However, once you insert a local SIM, the phone must be registered, or it will be blocked from the network after a short grace period.


    How to Register IMEI (For Foreigners)

    1. Declare your phone at customs when entering Uzbekistan (especially if new or over customs value limits).

    2. Register your device via one of the following:

    • Online: uzimei.uz, my.gov.uz, Birda app
    • In-person: Tashkent Airport desk, post offices, UZIMEI centers
    • USSD/SMS: Dial *1170# or SMS to 1170

    3. Pay the registration fee:

    • Within 30 days: ~82,400 UZS
    • After 30 days: ~103,000 UZS

    4. Complete biometric verification if required.

    5. Payment deadline: Within 10 days after applying.

    For how long can foreigners import their cars to Uzbekistan?

    Foreign citizens can enter Uzbekistan in their own car without paying duties and taxes for up to 90 days during one calendar year. However, it can only be used for non-commercial purposes. After the 90-day period, the car must be exported from the country.

    Bringing a Foreign Vehicle into Uzbekistan

    Foreign nationals are allowed to bring their personal vehicles into Uzbekistan without paying duties or taxes for a period of up to 90 days within a single calendar year. Please note that the vehicle may only be used for non-commercial purposes during this time.

    Extension Beyond 90 Days
    If you wish to keep your vehicle in Uzbekistan beyond the 90-day limit, you must apply for an extension. A daily fee is charged for this extension, calculated as follows:
    0.7% of the Basic Calculation Value (BCV) per horsepower of the vehicle per day.

    As of the current rate, the BCV is 330,000 UZS.
    For example:
    A car with 100 horsepower would incur a fee of:
    231,000 UZS per day
    6,930,000 UZS for 30 days (approximately $560 USD)

    Fee Calculation Formula:
    Extension Fee = BCV × 0.7% × Vehicle Horsepower × Number of Days

    Car Rental Option
    If you prefer not to bring your own vehicle, you can rent a car locally. Rental costs vary depending on the car type and rental company, but average between $400 to $1,000 per month.
